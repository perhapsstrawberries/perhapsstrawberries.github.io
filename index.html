01 / scRNA-seq · aging signatures

Aging Signatures in Familial Alzheimer's Organoids

Single-cell reanalysis of a public iPSC-derived cortical-organoid dataset (GEO: GSE301700) asking whether familial AD mutations — PSEN1ΔE9, PSEN1M146V, APPswe — drive aging-like transcriptional change. Pseudobulk DESeq2 across ~56k cells / 14 samples, scored against the 124 conserved aging genes (Jeffries et al., Nature 2025) per cell type. The aging core is significantly suppressed in excitatory and inhibitory neurons (padj 3e-7 / 5e-4), driven by PSEN1M146V and APPswe.

03 / ATAC-seq

HDAC1 Chromatin Accessibility in Dendritic Cells

End-to-end ATAC-seq pipeline in Nextflow DSL2 — from raw reads through QC, alignment, mitochondrial filtering, peak calling with MACS3, and DESeq2 differential accessibility. Integrates paired RNA-seq to map concordant chromatin and expression changes, reproducing published Figure 6 panels from the HDAC1 dendritic cell study.

Nextflow Bowtie2 MACS3 DESeq2 HOMER R RNA-ATAC

04 / tooling · CLI + PyPI

seqstatx — Sequence Statistics CLI

A fast command-line tool for FASTA/FASTQ summary statistics — N50, N90, GC%, and length distributions — on plain or gzipped inputs. Pure Python 3.10+ with zero dependencies, so it runs in any HPC or Conda environment without compiled binaries. Published to PyPI (pip install seqstatx) with a pytest suite and GitHub Actions CI passing across Python 3.10–3.13. Demonstrates packaging, testing, and release engineering alongside the analysis work.

About

I'm a computational biology graduate student at Boston University (M.S. Bioinformatics, expected 2026), currently working as a Graduate Research Assistant in the TCW Lab at BU School of Medicine — analysing single-cell RNA-seq datasets related to Alzheimer's disease and contributing to an interactive web interface for single-cell data exploration.

Before BU, I completed a double degree in Applied Data Science and Communication at Western Sydney University (Vietnam campus, 2024), where I developed a foundation in machine learning pipelines and large-scale data analysis. I also worked as a Data Analyst at VieON and as a Research Assistant with King's College London and Cardiff University.

I'm drawn to the engineering side of bioinformatics — reproducible pipelines, clean workflows, and analyses that actually hold up. My current interests are co-expression network analysis, multi-omic integration, and the molecular biology of neurodegeneration.
